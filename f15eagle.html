F-15 Eagle

Overview

The McDonnell Douglas F-15 Eagle is an American twin-engine, all-weather tactical fighter aircraft designed to gain and maintain air supremacy in aerial combat. First introduced in the 1970s, the F-15 has since established itself as one of the most successful and formidable fighter jets, with a highly impressive combat record. It is still in service with several air forces worldwide and has continuously evolved through various upgrades in avionics, weapons systems, and structural improvements.

Crash History

The F-15 Eagle has an impressive safety and combat record. While there have been accidents and incidents over the decades, particularly in training exercises, the F-15 remains an extremely reliable aircraft. In combat, it boasts an unmatched record, with over 100 confirmed air-to-air kills and zero losses. Any crashes that have occurred are primarily due to human error or mechanical failure unrelated to combat.
