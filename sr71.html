SR-71 Blackbird

Overview

The Lockheed SR-71 Blackbird was an advanced, long-range, Mach 3+ strategic reconnaissance aircraft developed by Lockheed's Skunk Works division. Built primarily from titanium to withstand extreme heat, the SR-71 was designed to operate at high altitudes and speeds, outpacing threats by outrunning them. Operational from 1966 to 1998, it remains one of the fastest and highest-flying jets in history, known for its sleek design and groundbreaking reconnaissance capabilities.

Crash History

During its service, the SR-71 Blackbird had several accidents and incidents, primarily related to the extreme demands placed on the aircraft's materials and systems. However, its speed and altitude capabilities allowed it to avoid combat losses. In total, 12 of the 32 SR-71 aircraft were lost in accidents, but none due to enemy action. Despite these losses, the SR-71 maintained a remarkable service record, contributing significantly to reconnaissance missions during the Cold War.
