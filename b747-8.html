Boeing 747-8

Overview

The Boeing 747-8 is the latest and largest version of the iconic Boeing 747, often referred to as the "Queen of the Skies." The 747-8 is designed for long-range flights and offers superior fuel efficiency compared to its predecessors. With its distinctive hump and four engines, it is capable of carrying up to 660 passengers in a two-class configuration. It is used primarily for international routes and is known for its spacious cabins and advanced technology.

Crash History

The Boeing 747-8 has a remarkable safety record. Since its introduction in 2012, the aircraft has been involved in very few accidents or incidents. Like all modern airliners, the 747-8 incorporates advanced safety features and has undergone rigorous testing and certification. The model continues to be one of the safest large aircraft in service, benefiting from the long-standing history and proven design of the Boeing 747 family.
